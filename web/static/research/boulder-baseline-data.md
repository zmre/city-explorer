### 2.2 Walk Score and Bike Score

**Walk Score: 58** (Somewhat Walkable)
- Most errands can be accomplished on foot in downtown/university areas
- Some neighborhoods are highly walkable (70-80+ in core areas)
- Suburban areas require a car

**Bike Score: 87** (Very Bikeable)
- Boulder is one of the most bike-friendly cities in America
- Over 300 miles of bikeways
- 96 miles of bike lanes
- "Gold" Bicycle Friendly Community (League of American Bicyclists)

**Transit Score: 35** (Some Transit)
- RTD bus service available
- Skip bus system connects to Denver
- Limited compared to major cities

**Source:** Walk Score® (walkscore.com), Boulder Transportation Master Plan, League of American Bicyclists

## 4. ADDITIONAL CONTEXT & CONSIDERATIONS

### 4.1 Climate Characteristics
- **Semi-arid continental climate** with low humidity
- **High altitude effects:** Intense sun (30% more UV), lower oxygen
- **Chinook winds:** Rapid temperature changes (50°F swings possible)
- **Weather variability:** "If you don't like the weather, wait 5 minutes"
- **Wildfire season:** Growing concern (June-September)
- **Hailstorms:** Notable risk in spring/summer

### 4.2 Lifestyle Factors
- **Outdoor-oriented culture:** Hiking, biking, climbing central to lifestyle
- **Health-conscious:** Organic food, fitness, wellness culture
- **Environmental awareness:** Strong sustainability focus
- **Progressive politics:** Liberal-leaning community
- **University town:** CU Boulder influences culture and demographics
- **Tech hub:** Growing startup and tech company ecosystem
- **"Boulder Bubble":** Perception of being isolated/insulated from broader issues

### 4.3 Challenges & Limitations
- **Housing affordability:** Major barrier to entry/living
- **Limited diversity:** 88% white population
- **Altitude adjustment:** Can take weeks/months for some people
- **Dry climate:** Nosebleeds, dry skin common
- **Wildfire smoke:** Increasing issue in summer months
- **Traffic congestion:** Growing problem, especially US-36
- **Limited nightlife:** Compared to larger cities
- **No ocean access:** Deal-breaker for water/beach lovers

## 6. CONCLUSION

Boulder, Colorado presents as a **premium mountain lifestyle destination** with exceptional climate, outdoor recreation access, and quality of life metrics. However, these benefits come at a significant cost premium, particularly in housing.

**Ideal For:**
- Outdoor enthusiasts (hiking, biking, climbing, skiing)
- Health and wellness-focused individuals
- Those who value education and culture
- Tech professionals and researchers
- People who thrive in low humidity, sunny climates
- Those willing to pay premium for mountain access and lifestyle

**Not Ideal For:**
- Budget-conscious individuals/families
- Those seeking ocean/beach access
- People sensitive to altitude or dry climates
- Those who prefer urban density and nightlife
- Individuals seeking cultural/ethnic diversity

**Baseline Summary:**
Boulder represents a high-quality, high-cost mountain lifestyle with exceptional outdoor access, excellent climate (245+ sunny days), and strong community amenities. The primary trade-offs are housing costs (239% of national average) and lack of ocean access (750+ miles). This baseline will be valuable for comparing other cities that may offer similar benefits at different price points or with different geographic advantages.
