## METHODOLOGY & DATA SOURCES

### Climate Data Sources
- **Primary:** World Meteorological Organization (WMO) databases
- **Regional:** European Climate Assessment & Dataset (ECA&D)
- **National:** AEMET (Spain), IPMA (Portugal), National Weather Services
- **Period:** 30-year climate normals (1991-2020)

### Population Data Sources
- National statistical offices (INE, ISTAT, Eurostat)
- 2023-2024 estimates
- Municipal/metropolitan area definitions

### Sunny Days Definition
- Days with <30% cloud cover OR
- Days with >6 hours of sunshine
- Based on meteorological station data

### Humidity Measurements
- Relative humidity percentages
- Seasonal averages (4 seasons)
- Ranges show min-max across year

### Geographic Proximity
- **Mountains:** Within 50km or visible from city
- **Ocean/Coast:** Coastal city or within 40km
- **"Near":** 35-50km from feature

## LIMITATIONS & NOTES

### Data Considerations
1. **Climate variability:** Year-to-year variations exist; data shows long-term averages
2. **Urban heat islands:** City centers may be 1-2°C warmer than reported airport stations
3. **Microclimates:** Specific neighborhoods may vary from city averages
4. **Population fluctuations:** Metropolitan definitions vary by country
5. **Sunshine measurement:** Different methodologies between countries (reconciled where possible)

### Research Scope
- **Included:** Western and Eastern Europe as defined by standard geographic boundaries
- **Excluded:** Northern Europe (insufficient sunny days), most of Central Europe (similar reason)
- **Eastern Europe:** Limited options meeting 200+ sunny days criterion (Black Sea coast primary)

### Future Considerations
- Climate change impacts may alter these patterns over time
- Data should be updated every 5-10 years as new climate normals are established
- Individual site visits recommended to assess microclimates and personal preferences
